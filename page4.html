Mince Pies!!!

You'll need

  • 1 large jar of mincemeat (about 600g)
  • 2 satsumas, segmented
  • 1 apple, finely chopped
  • zest 1 lemon
  • little icing sugar, for dusting

    • For the pastry:

  • 375g plain flour
  • 260g unsalted butter, softened
  • 125g caster sugar, plus extra for a cheeky sprinkle
  • 1 large egg, plus one beaten egg for a cheeky glaze

    • Let's get baking!

    • Place 375g plain flour and 260g softened unsalted butter in a large bowl, and rub together to a crumb consistency
    • Add 125g caster sugar and 1 large beaten egg, and mix together
    • Tip out onto a lighly floured surface and fold until the pastry comes together, but be careful not to over mix!
    • Wrap the pastry into cling film and chill in the fridge for 10 minutes
    • Scoop the large jar of mincemeat into a bowl and add two segmented satsumas, one finely chopped apple and the zest of one lemon
    • Roll out the pastry to 3mm thick
    • Using a round cutter, cut out about 16 bases, placing them into muffin trays. Put 1 and a half tbsp of mincemeat mixture into each. Brush the edge of each pie with a little beaten egg
    • Re-roll out the pastry to cut lids and press them on top of the mincemeat filled pies. Glaze with beaten egg, sprinkle with a cheeky bit of caster sugar and make a small cut in the tops or create your own design
    • Bake the mince pies for 15-20 minutes until golden brown, and dust with a little more icing sugar before serving!

      • To enhance the flavour, serve with a cheeky christmas joke: What did Santa do when he went speed dating? He pulled a cracker!
